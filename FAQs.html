What areas do you specialise in?

I am particularly experienced with the following kinds of texts:
  • Marketing materials: Brochures, leaflets, advert copy
  • Websites: One web page or a whole website, corporate blogs
  • Theses, dissertations & papers: PhD, Masters or Bachelor thesis – directly in Overleaf
  • Books & literature: Novels, illustrated books, non-fiction
Having studied linguistics and worked in more creative industries, I specialise in the following topics in particular:
  • linguistics & philology;
  • media & publishing;
  • marketing & communications;
  • watchmaking and luxury goods;
  • music.



Do you also translate into German or Russian?

No. An important principle of translation is that a translator only translates into their mother tongue. Though I have mastered German and Russian to a high level, they are not my native languages. Therefore, I only translate into English.



How much does it cost?

A lot of translators charge a minimum fee – but I only charge for what you need; I charge according to the number of words in the source text and the service required. My standard charges are as follows:

Translation: €0.12 per word
Editing: €0.06 per word
Proofreading: €0.03 per word

Please contact me for an exact quote.



What is the difference between editing and proofreading?

Proofreading mainly involves checking spelling and grammar. Editing is a bit like proofreading+: It involves checking and improving the consistency, accuracy and style of a text. This means, in addition to checking spelling and grammar, also checking that the information in it is correct. This is helpful if you have names and facts that you would like double checking, for example.



How long does it take?

This can depend on the project in question. As a rule of thumb, a professional translator can translate up to 2000–2500 words per day – but of course more complex texts can take longer.

If you have any questions about using translation memory systems and how they can help you, please do not hesitate to contact me.


Programming languages
