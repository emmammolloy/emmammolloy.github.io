Do you also translate into German or Russian?

No. An important principle of translation is that a translator only translates into their mother tongue. Though I have mastered German and Russian to a high level, they are not my native languages. Therefore, I only translate into English.



How much does it cost?

A lot of translators charge a minimum fee – but I only charge for what you need: I charge according to the number of words in the source text and the service required.

Translation: €0.12 (+€0.03 per word for source files in an uneditable format such as image or PDF.)
Editing: €0.06 per word
Proofreading: €0.03 per word

A translation of 500 words (approx. one full side of A4), for example, would cost €60; editing the same amount of text would be €30; proofreading would be €15. I can also provide a quote in GBP upon request.



What is the difference between editing and proofreading?

Proofreading mainly involves checking spelling and grammar. Editing is a bit like proofreading-plus: It involves checking and improving the consistency, accuracy and style of a text. This means, in addition to checking spelling and grammar, also checking that the information in it is correct. This is helpful if you have names and facts that you would like double checking, for example.



How long does it take?

This can depend on the project in question. As a rule of thumb, a professional translator can translate up to 2,500 words per day – but of course more complex texts can take longer.

I calculate my capacities on the basis that I can translate approximately 500 words of source text (or one full A4 page) per hour; edit 1,000 words (two A4 pages) per hour; proofread 2,000 words (five A4 pages) per hour.
